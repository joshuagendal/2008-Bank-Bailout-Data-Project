Explanation of Variables/Columns


On this page you will find explanations for all the variables you find in this project. You can think of variables as the data in the columns for all the tables.

ID

A simple identifier number from 1-434.

Party

Political Party - Dem is Democrat and Rep is Republican

State

U.S. State. It is worth noting that all of the tables for this project are ordered by state, and then state district ID number.

PAC $

Campaign contributions from Political Action Committees representing the financial services industry for the 2008 eleciton cycle.

Vote 1

The member's first vote on the 2008 EESA (bank bailout)

Vote 2

The member's second vote on the 2008 EESA (bank bailout)

Switch

This indicates whether or not the members changed their vote on the 2008 EESA (bank bailout)

Dist Supp

This is the result of a public opinion survey of constituents in the member's congressional district, asking respondents if they support or oppose the bank bailout of 2008. Dist Supp is the percentage of respondents in the member's district that answered they support the bailout.

Dist Opp

This is the result of a public opinion survey of constituents in the member's congressional district, asking respondents if they support or oppose the bank bailout of 2008. Dist Opp is the percentage of respondents in the member's district that answered they oppose the bailout.

ADA Score

ADA stands for Americans for Democratic Action. Each year, the institution picks 20 legislative votes dealing with hot-button social and economic issues, and assigns 5 points to the congressman or congresswoman if the public position they take on the vote is the liberal/Democratic position. The score essentially is an indicator for how liberal each member of Congress votes, and is the standard used in Political Science to quantify how ideologically liberal or conservative a member of Congress is. This score ranges from 0-100, 0 being the most conservative and 100 being the most liberal.

Margin of Victory

The member's margin of victory for the 2006 election.

Swing District

This indicated whether or not a member is in a "swing district", or a congressional district whose election continuously sees close races.
